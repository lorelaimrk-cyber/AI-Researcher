# Prompt 05: Headline and social

The last step. Run it on the finished article. Produces the title, subtitle, email subject,
and the two LinkedIn posts.

---

The article is final. Read `docs/distribution.md`, then produce the packaging. Match the
voice in `docs/style-guide.md` and obey the same hard bans (no "not X, but Y," no dash as
punctuation, no machine words).

Give me all of the following.

## Title (give 3 options)

Legible to both audiences, leads with the idea, no clickbait the piece does not pay off. Under
about sixty characters.

## Subtitle (give 2 options)

One line that sharpens the promise and earns the click. This is the Substack subtitle that
sits under the title.

## Email subject line (give 3 options)

Under about fifty characters so it survives on a phone. Leads with the idea, not the topic. No
emoji, no all caps. Mark which one you recommend and why.

## LinkedIn Post A, for publish day

The core claim as a standalone idea. Three to five short paragraphs. First line is a claim
that stands alone before the "see more" fold. It must be valuable even to someone who never
clicks. End with one soft line pointing to the full issue. Suggest where the link should go
(post body or first comment) and at most three specific hashtags.

## LinkedIn Post B, for two days later

A different angle on the same issue, usually the risk case or one surprising number, so it
reaches the readers who missed Post A. Same format rules as Post A.

Keep every option tight. I would rather have three strong title options than eight weak ones.
