# Prompt 03: Draft

Run this once you have chosen the angle. Claude writes the full issue in your voice against
the article template.

---

Draft the full issue on the angle I chose. Before you write, read `docs/style-guide.md` in
full and follow `templates/article-template.md` for the structure. Use only facts from the
research digest, each already sourced.

Write in the voice defined in the style guide. The essentials:

- Lead with the claim in the first two sentences. No warm-up.
- Plain words, short declarative sentences, reasoning from cause and effect.
- Calibrate your confidence at least once. Say how sure you are and why.
- Use the firm-as-a-machine lens where it helps.
- Carry a clear benefit case and a clear risk case. Steelman the side I disagree with.
- Land exactly one throughline, a single idea worth keeping, written as a statement under a
  heading called "The throughline." Do not label it a principle and do not phrase it as a
  command.
- Make one dated, falsifiable prediction and flag it so I can log it in the ledger.
- Close with "what I am watching next."

Obey the hard bans without exception:

- No "not X, but Y" constructions or any of that family.
- No dash used as punctuation. No em dash, no en dash. Use commas, colons, periods, or
  restructure. Use "to" for ranges.
- No machine vocabulary (delve, leverage as a verb, unlock, landscape, game changer, and the
  rest of the banned list in the style guide).
- No commands to the reader. No "you have to," "you must," "you need to," "you should," or
  bare imperatives. Reframe as observation or as a conditional outcome. Using "you" to mean
  the reader's firm inside a statement or question is fine.
- Every fact, number, quote, and firm action carries a named source and a link.

Length: aim for 800 to 1200 words. Long enough to reason, short enough to finish over a
coffee.

Leave one clearly marked placeholder, `[YOUR DETAIL HERE]`, at the natural spot where I
should add a specific thing only I would know: a number from a project, a phrase a client
used, a mistake I watched a firm make. Do not invent that detail. I will supply it.

Save the draft to `articles/<today-date>-<slug>.md` with a short frontmatter block at the top
recording the date, the pillar, and the working title.
