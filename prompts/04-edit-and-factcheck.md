# Prompt 04: Edit and fact check

Run this on the draft from prompt 03. This is the quality gate. It enforces the bans and
verifies every claim.

---

Edit the draft you just wrote. Work in two passes and report what you changed.

## Pass 1: Style enforcement

Go through `docs/style-guide.md` and fix every violation. Specifically:

- Search the text for " not " and remove every "not X, but Y" construction. Rewrite each as a
  direct claim.
- Search for the em dash, the en dash, and " - ". Remove every dash used as punctuation.
  Replace with a comma, colon, period, or a restructured sentence. Convert ranges to "to."
- Search for every banned machine word (delve, leverage as a verb, unlock, landscape, game
  changer, revolutionize, "it is worth noting," and the full list). Remove or replace each.
- Search for commands to the reader ("you have to," "you must," "you need to," "you should,"
  and bare imperatives). Reframe each as an observation or a conditional outcome.
- Confirm the keeper idea sits under "The throughline" as a plain statement and that the word
  "principle" appears nowhere in the piece.
- Confirm the piece leads with its claim, calibrates confidence at least once, and carries a
  benefit case and a risk case.
- Read it for rhythm. Break up any run of identical sentence lengths. Cut throat-clearing.

## Pass 2: Fact check

For every factual statement, number, quote, and firm action:

- Confirm it traces to a named primary source with a working link.
- Re-fetch the source if you are not certain the draft represents it correctly.
- Fix any claim the source does not actually support.
- Anything you cannot verify, cut it, or rewrite it as a clearly labeled opinion or estimate
  ("my rough estimate," "I have not confirmed this").

## Report

After both passes, give me:

- A short list of what you changed and why.
- The filled-in pre-publish checklist from the bottom of `docs/style-guide.md`, every box
  ticked or flagged.
- Any claim you had to soften or cut for lack of a source, named explicitly so I can decide.

Then save the cleaned version over the draft file. Keep the `[YOUR DETAIL HERE]` placeholder
in place for me.
