# Prompt 02: Angle selection

Run this against the digest from prompt 01. Claude proposes angles and recommends one. You
make the final call. This is the single decision that most decides whether the issue is sharp.

---

Using the research digest you just produced, propose three possible angles for this week's
issue. Read `docs/positioning.md` again so each angle serves the thesis: AI is repricing
engineering judgment.

For each of the three angles give me:

- **The claim.** One sentence stating the argument the issue would make. It must be
  falsifiable or at least arguable, not a summary of the news. "Firms are adopting AI" is not
  an angle. "Firms that keep billing by the hour for AI-assisted work will lose the bids
  within two years" is an angle.
- **The pillar.** Which content pillar it sits in.
- **Why now.** The specific story from the digest that makes this current this week.
- **The benefit case and the risk case** in one line each, so I can see the issue has both.
- **Who shares it.** Which reader forwards this to a colleague, and why.
- **The risk in the angle.** Where it could be wrong, thin, or too obvious.

Then recommend one, and say plainly why it beats the other two. Favor the angle that is most
ownable, most specific, and most useful to someone who runs a firm. Avoid the angle that
every other AI newsletter will also write this week.

Wait for me to choose before drafting. I may pick yours, combine two, or steer you somewhere
else.
