# Metrics and feedback

The newsletter is a machine. A machine without a feedback loop drifts. This file is the loop:
measure a few things that matter, learn from them, and adjust the calendar and the voice.
This is Dalio's evolve-by-feedback idea applied to your own publishing.

## Measure monthly, not daily

Daily numbers are noise. Once a month, log the figures below and read the trend. Spend more
time deciding what to change than collecting data.

| Metric | Where to find it | What it tells you |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| Subscribers, total and new | Substack dashboard | Is the audience growing |
| Open rate | Substack stats per issue | Is the subject line and the trust working |
| Click rate | Substack stats per issue | Is the email body pulling people to read |
| LinkedIn impressions and follows | LinkedIn analytics | Is distribution reaching new people |
| New subscribers attributed to LinkedIn | Substack source breakdown | Is the main channel converting |
| Replies and comments of substance | Inbox and threads | Are the right people engaging |

## The one qualitative signal that beats them all

Who replies, and what they say. One thoughtful reply from a partner at a target firm is worth
more than a hundred opens. Keep a short running note of who engages and what they cared
about. Those people are your future clients and your best topic radar.

## The monthly review, in fifteen minutes

1. Log the six numbers above into the table below.
2. Mark which pillar each issue used (from `docs/content-pillars.md`).
3. Note the two issues with the highest open and click rates. Which pillar, which angle?
4. Write one sentence: what will I do more of next month, and what will I drop?
5. Adjust `docs/editorial-calendar.md` to bias toward what pulled, while still covering all
   five pillars over the quarter.

## Running log

```
Month: ______
Subscribers (total / new): ______ / ______
Avg open rate: ______
Avg click rate: ______
LinkedIn follows gained: ______
Best issue (pillar, angle, open rate): ______
Worst issue (pillar, angle, open rate): ______
Notable readers who engaged: ______
Decision for next month (do more / drop): ______
```

Copy the block each month. Do not overwrite history. The trend is the value.

## Guardrails so the numbers do not corrupt the voice

Metrics are a compass, not a boss. Two rules keep them honest:

1. **Never trade the voice for a number.** If a clickbait subject lifts opens but cheapens the
   brand, do not keep it. You are building a long reputation with senior people, not chasing a
   weekly spike.
2. **Judge on a quarter, not a week.** One issue means nothing. A trend across a quarter means
   something. Change the strategy on trends, not on single data points.
