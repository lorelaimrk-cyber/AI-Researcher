# Positioning

## The one-line promise

A weekly, plainly reasoned read on how AI is changing the economics and the practice of
engineering consulting, written for the people who run these firms and sell their work.

## The reader

Two audiences, treated as one. They share a business model, so they share a problem.

- **AEC engineering consultancies**: civil, structural, MEP, geotechnical, infrastructure,
  and environmental firms. Names like Arup, AECOM, Jacobs, WSP, Stantec, Mott MacDonald,
  Ramboll. The reader is a principal, a discipline lead, a digital or innovation lead, or a
  partner who owns a profit number.
- **Engineering and digital practices inside management consultancies**: McKinsey, BCG,
  Bain, Accenture, Capgemini Engineering, Deloitte. The reader runs an engineering,
  digital, or AI transformation practice and sells expertise by the day.

Both sell judgment by the hour. Both bill for senior time. Both are now watching software do
a growing share of the work a junior engineer used to do. That shared exposure is the spine
of the newsletter.

## The ownable thesis

> AI is repricing engineering judgment. The firms that win will be the ones that change what
> they charge for before the market changes it for them.

Most coverage of AI in this industry describes tools. This newsletter follows the money:
what AI does to the billable hour, to the pyramid of junior and senior staff, to how a firm
prices and wins work, and to where liability sits when a model is in the loop. That is the
conversation the people with budgets actually care about, and few people are writing it
plainly.

## Why you

You are an AI expert who reads the research and the firm filings and translates them into
decisions a consulting leader can act on this quarter. You hold a point of view, you make
dated predictions, and you grade yourself in public. The reader trusts you because you show
your reasoning and you keep score.

## The contrarian edge

Pick a stance and hold it across issues so you become known for it. A strong candidate:

> The threat to engineering consultancies is not that AI replaces engineers. It is that AI
> collapses the price of the work that funds the training of the next generation of
> engineers. The pyramid is the product, and AI is melting its base.

State it as a claim, defend it with evidence each week, and update it openly when the
evidence changes. A clear, falsifiable position is what gets shared and remembered.

## Newsletter name candidates

Pick one that you can say out loud without wincing and that signals the beat.

1. **The Billable Hour** (the economic angle, memorable, slightly provocative)
2. **Judgment at Scale**
3. **The Engineering Margin**
4. **Load Path** (a structural-engineering term for how force travels to ground; doubles as
   a metaphor for how value travels through a firm)
5. **The Consulting Machine**
6. **Drawn by Machine**
7. **First Principles, Applied**
8. **The Senior Hour**

Recommendation: **The Billable Hour** or **Load Path**. The first is instantly legible to
both audiences. The second is distinctive and rewards the AEC reader who gets the reference,
while still working as plain English.

## The boundary

You write about the business and practice of engineering consulting under AI. You do not
write generic AI news, model release recaps, or consumer AI takes. When a story does not
touch how these firms make money, train people, deliver work, or carry risk, it does not
belong in the issue.
