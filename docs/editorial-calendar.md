# Editorial calendar

A twelve-week rolling plan. It is a default, not a cage. When a big story breaks, cover it
and push the planned topic forward a week. The point is to never face a blank page and to
guarantee you cover all five pillars in a quarter.

Each week names a primary pillar and a working angle. The weekly brief from `gather.py`
will surface the specific news that makes the angle current.

| Week | Pillar | Working angle |
|------|--------|---------------|
| 1 | Economics | What AI does to the billable hour. The flagship piece that states the thesis. |
| 2 | Delivery | What firms are actually running in production versus what they demo. |
| 3 | Risk | Who is liable when a model produced the calculation or the spec. |
| 4 | Talent | The broken training pipeline: how you make a senior when juniors no longer do junior work. |
| 5 | Market | What clients now do in house that they used to pay a consultancy to do. |
| 6 | Economics | Pricing under AI: why the fixed-price deliverable is coming back. |
| 7 | Delivery | Generative and AI assisted design: where it helps, where it quietly fails. |
| 8 | Risk | Governing model use without killing the upside. A practical playbook. |
| 9 | Talent | The new org chart: roles that appear and roles that vanish. |
| 10 | Market | AI native entrants and what they threaten first. |
| 11 | Economics | The margin question: where the money goes when timesheets shrink. |
| 12 | Quarter review | Grade the predictions ledger in public. Reweight the calendar from the metrics. |

## How to run it

- **Plan in batches.** Once a month, sit with the next four rows and the recent briefs and
  sharpen each working angle into a real question.
- **Stay two ahead.** Keep a backlog of two drafted issues so a bad week does not break the
  streak.
- **Let news override.** A genuine industry event beats the planned topic. Move the plan, do
  not abandon the discipline.
- **Reweight quarterly.** After week 12, use [metrics-and-feedback.md](metrics-and-feedback.md)
  to bias the next twelve weeks toward the pillars that pulled, while still touching all five.

## Evergreen reserve topics

Keep these ready for a slow news week. They do not depend on a fresh headline.

- The first principles of what a client actually buys from an engineering consultancy.
- A teardown of one firm's published AI strategy, read for what it reveals about its economics.
- What a young engineer should do now to stay valuable for twenty years.
- The history of a past automation wave in engineering (CAD, BIM) and what it predicts.
- A plain explainer of professional liability when AI is in the loop.
